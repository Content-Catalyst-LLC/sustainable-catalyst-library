# Research Quality Model — v3.5.0

## Purpose

The quality model identifies process readiness and missing governance work.

It is not:

- a truth score;
- a peer-review substitute;
- legal or ethical certification;
- proof that a Claim is correct;
- proof that evidence is sufficient;
- a guarantee of reproducibility.

## Dimensions and maximum points

```text
Research design                  15
Sources and citations            15
Claims and evidence              15
Provenance and reproducibility   10
Semantic organization             8
Pathways and navigation            7
Cross-product readiness            7
Governance and review             12
```

The raw maximum is normalized to 100.

## Research design

Signals include:

- explicit research question;
- objectives;
- methodology;
- scope and exclusions;
- project summary or body.

## Sources and citations

Signals include:

- included Research Sources;
- metadata verification;
- peer-review status;
- high and critical Source-integrity warnings.

Source type and peer-review status should be interpreted by discipline and research purpose.

## Claims and evidence

Signals include:

- structured Research Claims;
- Evidence Notes;
- Claim-to-Evidence connections.

The evaluator does not assess whether each Evidence Note logically proves a Claim.

## Provenance and reproducibility

Signals include:

- connected Knowledge Library documents;
- Source provenance records;
- project activity history;
- bibliography snapshots.

## Semantic organization

Signals include:

- canonical Knowledge Topics;
- reusable Concepts;
- Named Entities.

## Pathways and navigation

A project receives pathway credit when a Knowledge Pathway is derived from it.

This measures navigability, not research quality by itself.

## Cross-product readiness

Signals include:

- stable project identity;
- recorded cross-product handoffs.

A project can remain high quality without using every product.

## Governance and review

Signals include:

- assigned policies;
- completed or waived reviews;
- absence of unresolved high-severity issues;
- advancement into a formal review gate.

## Blocking rules

The readiness state becomes Blocked when:

- at least one critical issue is open; or
- at least one review outcome is Fail.

A high numerical score does not override these controls.

## Readiness bands

```text
Not ready             0–49
Needs review         50–69
Conditionally ready  70–84
Ready                85–100
```

## Re-evaluation

Projects should be re-evaluated after:

- adding or removing Sources;
- changing Claims or Evidence Notes;
- resolving Source-integrity notices;
- updating methods or scope;
- adding Topics or Concepts;
- creating a Pathway;
- completing a review;
- creating or resolving an issue;
- transitioning a governance gate;
- preparing a cross-product handoff;
- preparing a public release.
