# Finding Aid Publication Guide — v3.6.0

## Publication controls

A public finding aid requires:

- published collection;
- Public collection access;
- expired or absent embargo;
- enabled public finding aid;
- published public archive components;
- Public component access;
- expired or absent component embargo.

## Public fields

Finding aids may include:

- collection title and identifier;
- creator;
- institution;
- dates;
- extent;
- language;
- scope and content;
- arrangement;
- provenance;
- rights;
- component hierarchy;
- component summary, dates, extent, and access label.

## Private fields

Public finding aids omit:

- donor identity;
- accession agreements;
- private restrictions;
- custody-event details;
- internal digital-object URIs;
- checksums;
- Research Project IDs;
- Source IDs;
- internal disposition records;
- internal preservation findings.

## Editorial review

Before publication:

1. inspect every component title;
2. remove personal or confidential data;
3. review copyright and donor restrictions;
4. confirm embargo dates;
5. verify public access levels;
6. review scope and provenance wording;
7. test hierarchy on mobile;
8. test links;
9. test print output;
10. verify that empty levels do not confuse users.

## Citation

Use the collection’s preferred citation where available.

The public finding aid can be embedded with:

```text
[sc_archive_finding_aid id="123"]
```
