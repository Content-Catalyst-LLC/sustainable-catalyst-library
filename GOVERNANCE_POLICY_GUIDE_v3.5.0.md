# Governance Policy Guide — v3.5.0

## Research Policy records

Research Policies define reusable requirements that can be assigned to multiple Research Projects.

A policy should identify:

- the risk or quality domain;
- the governing principle;
- required controls;
- required evidence;
- the gate by which compliance must be reviewed;
- owner;
- version;
- effective date;
- review date;
- publication status.

## Lifecycle

```text
Draft
Active
Deprecated
Archived
```

Deprecated policies remain available for historical project records.

## Controls

The controls field is human-readable and can list one requirement per line.

Examples:

```text
Every principal Claim must have at least one reviewed Evidence Note.
Personal information must not appear in public exports.
Critical Source-integrity notices require project-level acknowledgement.
The final report must include stated limitations.
```

## Versioning

Create a new version when a policy change materially alters obligations.

Do not overwrite historical policy meaning when projects were approved under an earlier version.

## Public transparency

Mark a policy public only when its title, domain, version, and lifecycle status are appropriate for public disclosure.

The full policy body and control text are not automatically included in project transparency summaries.

## Assignment

Assign only policies that are applicable to the project.

A missing policy assignment reduces governance readiness but does not prove noncompliance.

## Review dates

The Governance Center flags policies whose review date is due within 30 days or has passed.

Policy review alerts do not change policy status automatically.
