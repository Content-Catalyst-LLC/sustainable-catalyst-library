# Knowledge Relationship Reference — v3.2.0

- **Related to** — meaningful general association.
- **Contrasts with** — materially different or opposing position.
- **Companion to** — records designed or curated together.
- **Broader than / Narrower than** — conceptual scope hierarchy.
- **Contains** — structural inclusion or aggregation.
- **Defines** — defines a Concept, Topic, term, or Entity.
- **Exemplifies** — is an example or case of another record.
- **About topic** — explicit subject relationship beyond taxonomy assignment.
- **Uses concept** — materially uses a Concept.
- **Mentions entity** — refers to a Named Entity.
- **Governed by** — constrained or framed by law, policy, standard, or institution.
- **Cites** — formally or substantively cites another record.
- **Supports** — supports a Claim, argument, method, or record.
- **Methodology for** — provides the method for another record.
- **Derives from** — derived from another record.
- **Summarizes** — summarizes another record.
- **Translates** — translated representation of another record.
- **Supersedes** — replaces an earlier record.
- **Precedes / Continues** — ordered sequence.

Use the narrowest supported relationship and explain it in the note. Weight 1–5 indicates editorial importance, not confidence or factual truth. Formal Research Source retractions and corrections remain governed by the v3.1.0 Source Integrity layer.
