# Evidence Publication and Review Guide — v2.7.0

## Direct quotations

Before publication:

- compare the quotation with the original source
- verify punctuation and omitted material
- verify the page, paragraph, section, figure, table, or timecode
- preserve surrounding context where needed
- distinguish quotation from paraphrase
- review copyright and fair-use considerations applicable to the publication

The plugin records research provenance but does not determine legal permission.

## Paraphrases

A paraphrase should not remain too close to the source wording.

Record the original locator and Source even when no words are quoted directly.

## Data points

Record:

- unit
- population
- geography
- time period
- denominator
- uncertainty
- table, figure, dataset row, or query context

The Evidence Note content can contain the value while the analytical note records interpretation.

## Claim status

### Draft

Not ready for review.

### Under review

Evidence and reasoning are being evaluated.

### Reviewed

Reviewed but not asserted as fully verified.

### Verified

The claim, scope, evidence relationships, limitations, and counterevidence have been reviewed.

### Disputed

Material disagreement remains.

### Retired

The claim is no longer used.

## Relationship discipline

### Supports

Evidence increases confidence in the claim.

### Contradicts

Evidence decreases confidence or directly conflicts with the claim.

### Qualifies

Evidence narrows scope, conditions, or strength.

### Contextualizes

Evidence provides relevant background without directly testing the claim.

### Illustrates

Evidence provides an example but not strong general support.

### Unresolved

The relationship remains uncertain.

## Strength

The 1–5 strength field is a structured reviewer judgment.

It should consider:

- directness
- methodological relevance
- source reliability
- independence
- scope match
- recency where relevant
- uncertainty

It is not an automatic statistical score.

## Public evidence packets

Before public release:

- remove private analytical notes not intended for publication
- confirm Source and document availability
- expose limitations and counterevidence
- avoid presenting link counts as proof
- confirm the project is public
- test mobile and print rendering
