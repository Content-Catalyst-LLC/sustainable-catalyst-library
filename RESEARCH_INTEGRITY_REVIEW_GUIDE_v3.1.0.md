# Research Integrity Review Guide — v3.1.0

## Corrected Sources

Determine whether the correction affects:

- bibliographic metadata only;
- a quoted passage;
- a data point;
- a method;
- a central conclusion;
- the project’s interpretation.

A metadata-only correction may require citation replacement without changing a Claim.

## Superseded Sources

Prefer the replacement for current guidance while retaining the older Source where historical comparison matters.

## Expressions of concern

Treat the Source as unresolved. Review high-stakes Claims, quotations, and public outputs before further reliance.

## Retracted or withdrawn Sources

Do not erase the historical record.

Review each dependent Claim and Evidence Note. Some records may remain appropriate for:

- documenting research misconduct;
- historical analysis;
- methodological critique;
- tracking a retraction itself.

## Archived Sources

Archived status is informational unless a current replacement is required by the research context.

## Project acknowledgement

Project decisions are local to the project. A Source can be replaced in one project and retained for historical analysis in another.

## Limits

The plugin does not determine truth, misconduct, legal validity, or whether a correction invalidates a conclusion. It exposes version and integrity evidence for human review.
