# Document Comparison Guide — v3.7.0

## Scope

Document comparison supports two to five Knowledge Library documents.

## Comparison fields

```text
Document summaries
Key points
Distinctive recurring terms
Shared recurring terms
Shared section labels
Pairwise term similarity
Topics
Concepts
Gap signals
```

## Pairwise similarity

The term-similarity score is:

```text
intersection of recurring terms
divided by
union of recurring terms
```

The result is normalized to 0–100.

## Interpretation

A high score may mean the documents use similar vocabulary.

It does not prove:

- agreement;
- shared methodology;
- equivalent evidence;
- common authorship;
- causal relationships;
- duplicate content;
- equal quality.

## Public comparison

Public comparison requires every included document to be public and have public document intelligence enabled.

## Recommended use

Use comparison to:

- identify related documents;
- reveal differing emphases;
- find shared Concepts;
- plan a Knowledge Pathway;
- develop Research Librarian follow-up questions;
- identify missing evidence or documentation;
- prepare a structured human comparison.
