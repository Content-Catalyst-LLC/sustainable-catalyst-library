# Retention and Disposition Guide — v3.6.0

## Retention classes

### Permanent retention

Use when the institution intends to preserve the collection indefinitely.

### Review before disposition

Use when appraisal is required at a future review date.

### Retain for specified years

Record both years and the triggering event.

Examples:

```text
Seven years after contract closure
Ten years after final publication
Five years after supersession
```

### Transfer

Use when custody should move to another authorized repository.

### Destroy after authorization

Use only where legally, ethically, and institutionally appropriate.

## Legal and administrative holds

A hold blocks approval and completion of:

```text
Transfer
Deaccession
Destroy
```

The plugin does not determine whether a legal hold should exist.

## Disposition workflow

```text
Proposed
Under review
Approved
Rejected
Completed
Cancelled
```

## Required review

Before approving disposition, verify:

- applicable law and regulation;
- contract or donor agreement;
- litigation or investigation holds;
- privacy obligations;
- public-record requirements;
- research-integrity obligations;
- institutional policy;
- preservation or historical value;
- evidence dependencies;
- approval authority.

## Destruction

The plugin records authorization and completion but does not perform file deletion.

Destruction should be executed through an approved operational process with independent verification.

## Historical record

Do not delete disposition history after completion.

The administrative record should remain available even when the underlying materials are transferred or destroyed.
