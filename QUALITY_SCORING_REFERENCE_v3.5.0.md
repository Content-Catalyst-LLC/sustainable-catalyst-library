# Quality Scoring Reference — v3.5.0

## Dimension states

Each dimension receives a relative state:

```text
Strong     at least 85 percent of available points
Adequate   at least 65 percent
Weak       at least 40 percent
Missing    below 40 percent
```

## Score interpretation

The score helps prioritize work.

Use it to ask:

- Which required project elements are absent?
- Which Sources need metadata or integrity review?
- Which Claims have no Evidence Notes?
- Is provenance sufficient to reconstruct the work?
- Can users navigate the project through Topics, Concepts, and Pathways?
- Are product handoffs carrying stable context?
- Have applicable policies and reviews been recorded?
- Are high-severity risks unresolved?

Do not use it to rank disciplines, researchers, institutions, or published findings.

## Known limitations

The v3.5.0 evaluator relies on structured Knowledge Library records.

A complete activity performed outside the platform may appear missing.

A populated field may still contain weak information.

A Source marked peer reviewed may still be unsuitable for a particular Claim.

A linked Evidence Note may not actually support the Claim.

A policy assignment does not prove the controls were satisfied.

## Human review

Human reviewers remain responsible for:

- domain judgment;
- evidence interpretation;
- methodological validity;
- ethics;
- privacy;
- legal analysis;
- conflict-of-interest review;
- final approval.
